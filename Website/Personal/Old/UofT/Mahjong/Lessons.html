What I Have Learned About Playing Mahjong

- Page Heavily Under Construction -

  • If you have good luck (for the day), any seat is a winning seat
  • If you have bad luck (for the day), any seat is a losing seat

  • When choosing seats, if a winner of a round is hot and has moved to a losing players seat, then the winner's chee will not be affected.
  • When choosing seats, if a loser of a round has moved to a wining players seat, then losing players chee will be neutralized.
  • It is not what you start with thats important, its how good you are at predicting future tiles; but starting with a good hand couldn't hurt.

  • Do Not be greedy, otherwise while going for a big hand, someone else will win big off one of your discards. Go smaller, every little win adds up.

  • All Luck + All Skills = Mahjong God; i.e. Quentin Liu, or Andy Lau in Fat Choi Spirit
  • All Luck + No Skills = Upredictable Competition
  • All Skills + No Luck = Very Angry Player Who Can Only Get Up To 4 Fan
  • No Luck + No Skills = Screwed

  • Drop Bombs Early, or pay the price
  • Not loosing; is winning; sometimes
  • better to win small, then lose big

  • Shaun's Sofa Seat is very hazardous to your MJ Chee

  • Game is not over till its over, so watch what you discard if you pick up the last tile.

  • Being very lucky at the beginning of the day, means that you will have used up all your chee by the end of the first round and will not have any left during the second round. Slow and steady wins the race.
